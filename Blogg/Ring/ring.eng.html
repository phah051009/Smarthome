Test: Ring Alarm 2.0 - good and easy-to-install home alarm

My rating: 4 out of 5 stars

A loud outdoor siren is the biggest change that the Ring Alarm 2.0 alarm system has undergone.

Tested product: Ring Alarm 2.0

Price: SEK 2,799 at Amazon Sweden

Ring is diligent in updating its product lines, which is not least noticeable in the number video doorbells on the market (seven at the time of writing). It is therefore not a big surprise that they are launching a 2.0 version of their alarm package just over six months after the first package was released.

Everything has become smaller

Most of the components basically have the same functions as before and the most noticeable difference is that most things have become slightly smaller.

Of the five parts included in the basic package, the base station is the same and the range extender basically has same size but new design. However, the keypad, motion detector and contact sensor have all shrunk something.

The keypad has also been redesigned and fitted with three new buttons. With these buttons it is possible to manually activate an alarm siren or send a notification to three preset contacts. As before goes it also to activate or deactivate the alarm with three different modes; Disarmed, Home and Away. The buttons for these have been made clearer and easier to press.

The fact that the news is few certainly does not do much, as Ring Alarm was already functioning well in the first the version. But it's a bit of a shame that all the negatives we had in our first test remain. Admittedly, the price has decreased somewhat (2,799 kroner now compared to previously 3,300 kroner), but the limited the setting options and the need for a paid subscription to get full functionality are still there.

Loud and ugly outdoor siren

The big news is instead the launch of a complementary outdoor siren, the Ring Alarm Outdoor Siren. Large both figuratively and literally, as it measures 23.5 x 23.5 x 5.7 centimeters and weighs a whopping 1.3 kilo (including batteries). The siren is mounted outdoors on the facade and howls (up to just over 100 dB) and then flashes red when an alarm is triggered upon detected motion/intrusion (or if you manually trigger the alarm).

Installing it is tricky, as a certain amount of measuring, drilling and screwing is required. If you settle for pure battery operation is easier, but if you want to connect power it becomes trickier.

Once in place, the Ring Alarm Outdoor Siren works just as easily and smoothly as the other Ring components The alarm system. It is possible to set how loud it should sound (four different modes) and how long it should last let (from one to ten minutes). And of course also when and how it should be activated.

Ring Alarm Outdoor Siren works well, does what it is supposed to and is a welcome addition to the siren that is built into the base station. The only objection we have is to its appearance.

For Ring has chosen to shape it as a giant version of their baby blue logo. A logo that honestly is not very pretty. We understand that the idea is probably that this blue lump should have a deterrent purpose and warn that "here is the alarm", but to at least have been able to choose a color using different stickers would have been desirable. Now it is a large and baby blue octagon on the facade that applies and nothing else.

Specifications

Product: Ring Alarm 2.0

Tested: December 2022

Manufacturer: Call

System requirements: Router, smartphone with Android or IOS

App in Swedish: No

Alarm siren: Yes (104 dB)

Battery time backup battery: 24 hours

Included in the basic package: Base station with built-in siren speaker, a keypad, a motion detector, a range extender and a door/window contact sensor.

Other: Subscription cost for Ring Protect Plus 10 euros/month. Support for surveillance cameras from Ring.

Price: SEK 2,799 at Amazon Sweden, SEK 900 for the outdoor siren at Amazon Sweden.
