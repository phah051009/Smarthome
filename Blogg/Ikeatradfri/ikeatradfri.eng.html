Ikeas smart LED lights - remote control in app or with voice

They can be controlled using a timer, in the app or with your voice, just to name a few options

For everyone, remote-controlled lights may not be desirable, but when Ikea is now investing more in the smart home are the lamps that form the basis because it is smart speakers and smart roller blinds that have been added the growing supply. In its basic design, the smart lamp is relatively simple. A remote control sounds turn on and off and dim the light bulb. Depending on which lamp applies, you can also change color tone, between a couple of different warm and cold tones or in the case of the one who can choose more between about twenty colors from the entire rainbow.

So far, the dimming function is certainly an advantage, because it works regardless of the fixture, but otherwise the function is much like a normal lamp with a lamp button on the wall. For more features and remote control requires that you invest in Ikea's gateway Wireless. It is connected directly with an ethernet cable to your wifi router and is the prerequisite for being able to control your lights with Ikea's app and not only with the help of remote control. It is also Wireless gateway that makes it possible to connect to Google Home/Assistant and to Apple Homekit if you want it.

Problematic installation

We have previously tested Ikea's smart roller blind and just like the roller blind, it is an Ikea Wireless gateway which is required to connect the lights so that they can be controlled with Ikea's mobile app and with Google Assistant. Just like the roller blind, the lights, remote controls and gateway are a lot of mess before we finally manage to get them all talking to each other. In any case, they must be connected by pressing a series of button presses on the remote control and then holding it inside two centimeters from the lamp to be connected to the system. And before that you must have connected Wireless gateway directly to an ethernet port on your Wifi router at home.

When everything is up and running, you can control the lighting from Ikea's app as long as you are connected the same wifi as the lights and via Google Home and Assistant in addition when you are anywhere where there is internet. The lights can also be controlled with a motion sensor so you can, for example can make the light in the storage room turn on automatically when you enter it.

Collected in app

In addition to the lamps, Ikea's app also collects the roller blind we previously tested and Ikea's speakers as a man launched together with Sonos. In addition to turning the lights on and off, you can also control them a timer function in the app. It works so that you can simulate that you are at home when you o the actual work is removed and it can also be set so that, for example, the lights slowly fades in before you wake up and so that the blinds go up when it's time to go up. The three different timer functions that are available are called Good Morning, Away and Light and darkness, but in practice all three have roughly the same function. Good morning is to wake you up at a specified time and because the timer will go away simulate that you are at home as usual when you are on a trip, it has the same function. Which makes it a bit redundant. Same thing with the last timer function Light and Dark, because even it only has the basic functions of setting fixed times and not as one might think, some connection to when the sun rises and sets.

More opportunities at Google

Via Google Assistant, I can request that the lights be turned off or on all or each individually and in the Google Assistant app you can also dim or change the color of the lights based on on what is available in each lamp. Funnily enough, Google gives me more than twice as many color options like the Ikea app does with the exact same lamp.

In addition to these ways of controlling and managing the lights, they also support Apple Homekit, which allows you to use Siri and the Home app in the iPhone to control the lights and also that you can have the Apple Tv as something of a connection center for the lights and other smart gadgets with Homekit support. It still feels, however, that the Ikea lamps could be significantly easier for the common man to actually get to work as intended and then should have more opportunities to be controlled by various chains of events. So that they simply become a little smarter and less frustrating.

Facts Ikea smart led lights Ikea Wireless

Function: Remote controlled lights

Remote control: Via separate remote control, or with gateway via Ikea's app or Google Home

Price: SEK 69-199 (the lamps), SEK 99 (Ikea Wireless signal amplifier), SEK 199 (Ikea Wireless remote control), SEK 299 (Ikea Wireless gateway). Individual parts are also sold in different packages, which can give a better price.
