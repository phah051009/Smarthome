Sonos Era 300: Incomparable sound in the new luxury model with built-in Dolby Atmos

My rating: 4 out of 5 stars

The speaker currently on everyone's lips: the multiroom speaker Sonos Era 300 with six built-in elements directed in different directions promises a listening experience called duga. We've tested!

Sonos Era is a solid piece with a match weight of 4.47 kilos. The design can obviously be experienced by some as somewhat doubtful: "Does it really look like that?" is the first comment I am served from one curious acquaintance who wants to view the manufacturer's latest creation. He is referring to the hourglass-like shape which is undeniably special. According to Sonos, the acoustic construction is optimized to deliver a as spacious and enveloping as possible, so you just have to like the position - if, like me, you are somewhat skeptical of the design.

It thus becomes something of a challenge to bestow the speaker with optimal placement, above all in view of that it should have a free play space of at least 20 centimeters all around.

High quality feeling

Just as always with products from Sonos, the feeling of quality in the finish is high. The speaker cabinet's matte white surface is pleasant to the touch. At the front edge on the top of the speaker is a longer, sunken slider used to adjust the volume, as well as a bunch of accurate touch-sensitive ones buttons with which you activate the voice control service, change tracks and pause or resume the playback. Sweet!

Just like on the recently released sibling Era 100, there is a usb-c port on the back which can be used to via 3.5 mm aux cable connect an external audio source alternatively to connect the speaker via network cable to your router. There is also a button to turn the microphone on/off. Like everyone multiroom speakers, stable wireless communication is of the utmost importance: the Era 300 supports Wi-Fi 6 (802.11a/b/g/n/ac/ax 2.4 or 5 GHz) as well as bluetooth (5.0) if for some reason you prefer it.

Easy installation, user-friendly interface

Installing Sonos speakers is a no-brainer, and so is the Era 300. Once you've fitted it with power it appears in the app, follow the instructions and it's done. If you have a Sonos configuration, it is just place the speaker in one of your existing rooms and proceed to various settings for as good a listening experience as possible.

For optimal playback quality, use the "Trueplay" function to set playback sound. You can choose between a quick or a more advanced adjustment, where the former also works for Android users. For the more advanced adjustment, as before, an IOS device is required.

Angled speaker elements

In the app there is a lot more to be done before you are set to go. If you want to voice control the speaker is it applies to Sonos' own assistant, Sonos Voice, or Amazon Alexa. Then it's time to add possible music services, in and of themselves not necessary as the speaker supports Airplay 2 as well Spotify Connect, but if you want everything collected in the Sonos app, there is still the option and most of the big ones the suppliers are represented. In the app, as in all Sonos speakers, you can adjust both bass and treble, for the Era 300 also the height of the sound, likewise if it should automatically identify and play back spatially (spacious) sound.

A fantastic sound experience

The equipment in the Era 300 consists of six digital class d amplifiers, two woofers angled to the left and right respectively to provide support for stereo sound as well as four tweeters – one forward-facing, two side-facing as well as an upward, placed in a directional horn to reflect the sound towards the ceiling when playing Dolby Atmos content.

In other words, there is a lot of technology here and it is not because a lot of expectations are built up when it's time for the speaker to declare a suit. My invited guests and I are looking for a couple spatial track on Amazon Music (Unilimited) and prepares us for an experience out of the ordinary. Trust case with Pink kicks off the bonanza and sure enough it sounds fantastic. The soundscape is wide and enveloping and I no need to look for a specific sweet spot, it sounds just as good no matter where in the room I place myself. Wow, like!

We continue with I'm Good with David Guetta and Bebe Rexta and the result is the same... magically good! The bass reproduction is captivating and the mid-range is rudely accurate. Track after track and Era 300 continues to deliver!

I turn off the spatial function to compare and it sounds...just as good! I shift back and forth though neither I nor any of my guests can notice any difference whatsoever. Ok, shit the same – sounds like it well, that sounds good, but the mind is certainly starting to wander; have we done something wrong, the files are not compatible with spacious sound? Well, everything is correct and even when we feed the speaker with the same track, but as lacks support for Dolby Atmos, it sounds just as good. What exactly is the deal with spatial sound?

More to look forward to

As for home theater sound, fine, I understand and when we get another copy of the Era 300 we will it will be really exciting to test them as surround speakers and in a true stereo configuration, with separated left and right channels. How good will that sound?

As a single speaker tasked with playing music, the Era 300 performs superbly, with a soundstage wider than a barn wall, but if it has to do with spatial sound is currently impossible for me to decide.

The grading is tricky in this case, just under 6,000 kroner for a speaker is a heck of a lot money and given that I was not able to fully test the concept – two units as a stereo pair or as surround speakers in a home cinema setup – it's even more difficult. At the same time, Era 300 has served some of the best sound experiences I've ever experienced from a single multiroom speaker.
