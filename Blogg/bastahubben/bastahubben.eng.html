Control the whole home with the same app

Anyone who has ever sat down and tried to realize this flexible, technology-filled existence knows that it is not It's always so easy to get the gadgets talking to each other. With more and more fun smart home gadgets to fill our houses with there is a space in the market for so-called control units or hubs.


These act as a connection center for all our connected curtains, lights, fans, heat pumps, and more. With the associated app, we can easily control all devices at the same time and set up smart scenes where each gadget behave exactly as we want automatically.


A well-functioning hub must therefore be able to control all of your smart gadgets. Here there are slightly different protocols that smart devices use. In addition to the technologies that we have known since before, such as wi-fi, bluetooth and infrared light, the protocols Z-Wave and Zigbee have also become big on the market. The more protocols your hub supports, the more potential it has to control your entire smart home.

Different hubs for different purposes

There are several aspects to look out for when it comes to choosing the right controller for your smart home, as hubs target different types of users. Maybe you're just looking for a smooth way to in the same interface to control all your smart lighting, or are you looking for a hub that lets you create more advanced automation scenes?


Perhaps the answer is that you need two different controllers for different purposes. Several of the hubs in this test for example, complement each other really well, as one of them can offer broad compatibility with, for example many different products, and another has invested in advanced automation options in the software.

So we did the test

In our test, we have selected six of the most popular smart control units that have been launched in the Swedish language market and which can be used to connect and control your smart home. They all have different characteristics and functions have different price range.


The controllers have been tested in the same apartment with a set of different smart lights, sockets, media players and sensors. We've rated them based on how well they do what they claim to do, affordability, compatibility and ease of use.

Google Nest Hub: smooth - when it works

My rating: 3 out of 5 stars

Nest Hub is an affordable and easy-to-use hub that is perfect for technology enthusiasts as well as your parents. But even though it works great as a control unit and offers unique functions compared to its competitors, it lacks it fully automation functions. The unstable experience further contributes to it not really being one full score in our eyes.

Connections: Wifi 802.11b/g/n/ac (2.4 GHz/5 Ghz) Wi-Fi, Bluetooth 5.0. LE
